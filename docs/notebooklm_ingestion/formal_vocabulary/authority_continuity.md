# Authority Continuity

Authority continuity is the persistence of a coherent control boundary from approval through execution.

It asks whether the same authority assumptions still govern the mutation boundary now.

Authority continuity can break even when identities and records remain unchanged, because runtime governance surfaces evolve.

Implications:
- Historical approver identity is insufficient.
- Present boundary coherence is required.

Contradiction:
Approval provenance is intact while execution boundary semantics changed.

Distributed-systems connection:
Comparable to leadership epoch change: prior authority record exists, present commit authority differs.
