# Legitimacy Half-Life

Legitimacy half-life is the conceptual interval after which confidence in authorization-to-runtime continuity materially decays.

It is shaped by volatility, coupling depth, and delay.

Implications:
- Different mutation classes have different half-lives.
- Delay without revalidation increases uncertainty monotonically.

Contradiction:
Assuming equal legitimacy at T+0 and T+7d under non-trivial runtime change.

Distributed-systems connection:
Comparable to freshness windows in time-sensitive coordination models.
